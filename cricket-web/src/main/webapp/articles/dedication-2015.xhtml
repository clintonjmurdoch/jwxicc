Willowfest 2015 - Round 4 v Hutt River Dedication - The Preview

Willowfest 2015 - Round 4 v Hutt River Dedication - The Preview

Match Facts

December 29, 10am, Mildura Senior College

History

Returning to Mildura after a two year absence, Johnnie Walker XI has added motivation to take home the 2015 title. Since the agonising 1 run loss in 2012 which ultimately consigned them to being beaten by Hutt River in the overall standings, the Principality has gone on to taste premiership success, taking out last year’s B Grade title. Obviously impressed with the two rivals’ storied history, Willowfest organisers have given the match marquee status by scheduling it at the beautiful Mildura Senior College main ground. A strong Johnnie Walker squad vs. an experienced Dedication core promises an enthusing battle, particularly with a potential grand final spot on the line.

Look out for...

The numbers Michael Dann has generated against Hutt River make for sexy reading. A long absence from any level of cricket may count against him, but simply stepping onto the field might be enough to petrify the opposition and extend his dominance.

Matt Barclay’s overall Willowfest numbers are solid, however his record vs. Hutt River with the bat shows a top score of 28 and just 3 runs in 4 other innings. With a bit of local form in the bank, expect Barclay to prove his class if he chooses to use himself up the order.

Brendan McCarthy took the ball in 2012 at an impossible time, and bowled so tightly that a jittery Johnnie Walker XI lower order crumbled. The haul of 5/13 was considered Hutt River’s finest spell in their 10 year history. Like Dann, past performances may be enough to succeed if he is not shown respect.

An unknown coming into the 2012 encounter, Haydn Falconer showed plenty of all-round potential and delivered a tricky repertoire immediately prior to McCarthy’s star turn. Since then, he has taken out an MDP award and become a mainstay of Hutt River’s team.

Team News

JWXI

A 12 man squad has been named for the carnival. Simon O’Shea’s first appearance since the 2011 premiership is in doubt but selectors have been working overtime in getting him across the line. Travis Murdoch returns for his first game in six years – a record – after answering the call to cover O’Shea. Josh Pearce gains his first baggy red and could make an immediate impact based on a solitary pre-carnival net session. Damien Taylor, Pete Barclay, Ben Mason and Pat Lahn will miss after featuring in 2012.

Squad: Matt Barclay (c), Chris Barclay, Michael Dann, Andrew Dowell, Colin Finn, Vaughan Garner (wk), Clinton Murdoch, Travis Murdoch, Josh O’Dwyer, Simon O’Shea, Josh Pearce, Warren Sidebottom.

Hutt River Dedication

Only 9 players have been listed by Hutt River, however they are typically able to find numbers when necessary. Jarryd Mills’ brother Jack Leo a.k.a. Leo Jack will meet Johnnie Walker XI for the first time, while McCarthy makes his first Willowfest appearance in three years to bolster the attack; just in time to face his favourite opposition. Warren Sparkes will play his first Johnnie Walker XI match in five years.

Interim Squad: Tyler Hankinson (c), Jarryd Mills (wk), Jack Leo, Joel McMahon, Haydn Falconer, Adam Burke, Brendan McCarthy, Warren Sparkes, Ian McMahon

Conditions

Remarkably, sunny conditions are forecast for the duration of the carnival. At the showpiece ground, the pitch is likely to be a belter. The match is expected to be played in 36̊C heat with a 5% chance of rain (note that there is a 5% margin of error).

Statistics

· Head to Head: Hutt River 3-2

· 50 Over Matches: Tied 2-2

· Last meeting: Hutt River 113 off 39.3 (J McMahon 24, Dann 5/23, M Barclay 2/20) defeated Johnnie Walker XI 112 off 45.3 (Garner 36, Dann 32, B McCarthy 5/13, Sluiter 3/15) at Onion Patch.

· First meeting at venue

· Seven players remain from the five past encounters: Matt Barclay, Garner and Murdoch for Johnnie Walker XI and Hankinson, Mills, McMahon and Sluiter for Hutt River. Sluiter will miss this year

· The average first innings score for 50 over matches is just 97. Hutt River bucked the bat first curse in the epic 2012 match

· Dann’s bowling average against Hutt River ballooned over 30% after taking 5/23 last time round

· Clinton Murdoch starts the carnival needing 45 for 500 career runs

· Hankinson and Mills have shared the Hutt River games record since the 2012 carnival. It is expected that one of them will soon miss a game with a crowbar-related leg injury
